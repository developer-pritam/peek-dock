Features

Everything you'd expect.
Nothing you don't.

Lightweight, event-driven, and privacy-focused. No background uploads. No telemetry. Just previews.

👁️
Instant hover previews

Preview panel appears the moment you hover a Dock icon — no clicking, no waiting. Driven by Accessibility events, not polling, so CPU usage is near zero at idle.

⊞
Multi-window grid

Apps with many open windows auto-arrange into a clean grid (up to 4 columns, 3 rows). Minimized windows are clearly badged.

⚡
One-click switch

Click any thumbnail to bring that exact window to front — including minimized or off-screen windows on other Spaces.

🔒
Private by design

Thumbnail captures stay entirely on-device. No analytics, no network calls. The app lives in your menu bar and stays out of the way.

How it works

Three steps.
That's it.

1

Hover any app in your Dock

Move your cursor over any running app icon in the macOS Dock. PeekDock listens using native Accessibility events — no polling, no CPU drain.

2

Preview panel appears

A floating panel pops up just above the icon, showing live thumbnail previews of every open window for that app — including minimized ones. Multiple windows are shown in a scrollable grid.

3

Click to switch

Click any thumbnail and PeekDock brings that exact window to the front, even if it's on a different Space or minimized. Move your mouse away and the panel quietly disappears.

Transparency

Why we need
two permissions.

PeekDock needs exactly two macOS permissions — no more. Here's precisely what each one is used for.

♿

Accessibility

Used to observe the Dock process for hover events and read which app icon your cursor is over. Without this, the preview panel cannot appear. No other app data is accessed.

🎥

Screen Recording

Used to capture thumbnail images of each window using Apple's ScreenCaptureKit framework. Frames are rendered in-memory only and never saved to disk or sent anywhere.
